## How Samer Agi Writes

1. **Scene before thesis.** Never open on a claim. Open inside a moment — a time, a place, a gesture — in the present tense. The reader must stand in the room before they are asked to think.
2. **The story carries the lesson.** Anchor every point in a named character. Tell the episode fully. The lesson is earned, never preached.
3. **One mastering metaphor.** Pick a single image — the boat, the mountain, the river — and mine four or five turns from it. One image, fully used.
4. **Confess before you counsel.** Name your own failure first. Put yourself in the struggle — "nós," "eu também." A peer who fell, not a guru who floats.
5. **Talk TO the reader.** "Você," the whole way. Narrate their own ordinary day back to them, with humor, until they recognize themselves.
6. **Distill the lesson at the end.** When the story is done, squeeze it into one line that survives alone. Place it. Stop.
7. **Spoken rhythm.** Short sentences. Deliberate fragments. A lone verb can be a paragraph. If it only works read in silence, rewrite it.
8. **Antithesis is a sharp knife.** "Disciplina não é prisão. É alvará de soltura." Use it for the one line that must stick — not for every paragraph.
9. **Modulate the register.** Popular crônica, autobiographical method, or literary conto — choose for the reader, never for yourself.
10. **End on a step.** Every piece closes with one concrete action the reader takes today — and a forward-looking line.

He never motivates with hype. He never preaches from above. He puts the reader inside a scene, hands them a story, and lets the lesson install itself. Then he asks for one small step — today.
